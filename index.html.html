About

Executive Leader & Recognized Authority
in Commercial Fraud & Financial Crime

Andrew J. La Marca, CFE, CAMS is the Vice President of Fraud & Data Management at Dun & Bradstreet, overseeing global functions spanning multiple time zones across Fraud, Research, Managed Services, and Data Management. With more than 15 years of experience spanning Capital One, Ally Financial, and D&B, Andrew has built one of the most recognizable careers in commercial fraud prevention in the United States.

As D&B's Global Fraud Data Owner, Andrew leads the development and continuous improvement of the firm's enterprise-wide fraud risk operating framework — deploying artificial intelligence, machine learning, and robotic process automation to detect and prevent emerging threats. He oversees a global organization covering multiple fraud use cases across time zones, with deep specialization in fraud risk intelligence, control design, and advanced analytics. Andrew also serves as an AI Champion for Dun & Bradstreet, leading organizational strategy for AI enablement and modernization — driving the adoption of intelligent automation, agentic workflows, and AI-native data architectures across global operations.

Andrew holds the COSO/ACFE Fraud Risk Management Certificate (2025), reflecting his commitment to evolving best practices. He serves on the ACFE Chapter Leaders Committee and is the sitting President of the ACFE Lehigh Valley Chapter — consistently demonstrating commitment to the broader anti-fraud profession beyond his corporate role.

A graduate of The Pennsylvania State University (B.S., Crime, Law & Justice, 2009), Andrew obtained his CFE in 2014 and CAMS in 2018. He is a contributing author to DigitalCXO and the Information Security Media Group (ISMG), and a recurring expert voice at conferences, podcast studios, and government-sponsored fraud forums nationwide.

What Andrew Speaks On

B2B Fraud & Business Identity Theft

How actors steal, reactivate, and exploit corporate identities to obtain credit and government funds — and the data-driven methods to stop them.

🤖
AI-Enabled & Deepfake Fraud

The emerging threat of generative AI in commercial fraud — synthetic documents, deepfake executives, AI-generated entities, and evolving detection strategies.

Strengthening Know Your Business controls — hidden vulnerabilities in commercial onboarding workflows, data decay, and fraudster exploitation of verification gaps.

Fraud × AML Convergence

The operational overlap between fraud and financial crime compliance — breaking silos, sharing intelligence, and building unified typology libraries.
